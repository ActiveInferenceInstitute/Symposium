## 4. Active Inference & Related Research

- **Association with Active Inference:**
  - Karl Friston is the *originator and key architect* of the *free energy principle* and *active inference* framework, which formalizes perception and action as minimizing a free energy bound on surprise [UCL Neuroimaging](https://www.fil.ion.ucl.ac.uk/~karl/)[1].
  - Key publications detail active inference as a unifying theory of brain function, linking Bayesian inference and control theory [Nature Reviews Neuroscience (2010)](https://doi.org/10.1038/nrn2787)[3].

- **Adjacent Fields:**
  - Bayesian brain hypothesis, computational psychiatry (dysconnection hypothesis in schizophrenia), neuroimaging analytics.
  - Mathematical frameworks: variational Bayesian methods, stochastic filtering, hierarchical models [PeerJ](https://peerj.com/kfriston/)[4].

- **Methodological Overlap:**
  - Modeling neurodynamics with generative models and predictive coding.
  - Simulations of neuronal interactions and functional integration [UCL Profile](https://profiles.ucl.ac.uk/2747-karl-friston)[7].

- **Applications:**
  - Computational psychiatry, understanding neurological disorders.
  - Machine learning and AI models inspired by brain's inferential processes [Serious Science Interview](https://serious-science.org/karl-friston-the-hallmark-of-a-good-scientist-is-asking-questions-that-no-one-has-asked-before-9668)[5].

- **Research Gaps:**
  - Ongoing efforts to refine the free energy principle's mathematical formalism and empirical validation.
  - Bridging theoretical models with experimental data remains a key challenge [OHBM Blog](https://www.ohbmbrainmappingblog.com/blog/karl-friston-enabler-during-the-week-theorist-on-the-weekend-part-1)[3].

## 1. Academic Background

### Educational History

Sander Van de Cruys holds a PhD in Psychology from the University of Leuven (KU Leuven), Belgium. His doctoral research focused on predictive processing and its application to autism and aesthetic experience. He completed his PhD under the supervision of Prof. Johan Wagemans in the Brain & Cognition Unit at KU Leuven, with his thesis titled *"Predictive Processing in Autism and Aesthetic Experience"* (2017) [1, 3].

- **PhD in Psychology**, KU Leuven, 2017 [1, 3]
- **Master’s in Psychology**, KU Leuven, 2012 [1]
- **Bachelor’s in Psychology**, KU Leuven, 2010 [1]

### Research Areas & Expertise Domains

Van de Cruys specializes in:
- Predictive processing and active inference in cognition and emotion [1, 3, 4]
- Computational psychiatry, especially autism spectrum disorder [1, 3, 5]
- Experimental psycho-aesthetics and the cognitive science of aesthetic experience [1, 3, 4]
- Epistemic emotions (curiosity, insight, aha experiences) [3, 6]
- Belief systems, including conspiracy theories and their psychological underpinnings [5, 6]
- Top-down influences in visual perception [2]
